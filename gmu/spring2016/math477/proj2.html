Part 2

For part 2, we used the following equations.

\begin{align} (x-A_1)^2+(y-B_1)^2+(z-C_1)^2&=[c(t_1-d)]^2\\ (x-A_2)^2+(y-B_2)^2+(z-C_2)^2&=[c(t_2-d)]^2\\ (x-A_3)^2+(y-B_3)^2+(z-C_3)^2&=[c(t_3-d)]^2\\ (x-A_4)^2+(y-B_4)^2+(z-C_4)^2&=[c(t_4-d)]^2 \end{align}

Subtracting the last three equations from the first equation yields three linear equations in the form $$x \vec{u}_x + y \vec{u}_y + z \vec{u}_z + d \vec{u}_d + \vec{w} = 0$$

If we multiply out our first equation $$(x-A_1)^2+(y-B_1)^2+(z-C_1)^2=c^2(t_1-d)^2$$ it should yield $$x^2-2A_1x+A_1^2+y^2-2B_1y+B_1^2+z^2-2C_1z+C_1^2=c^2 t_1^2 d + c^2 d^2$$

If we do the same thing with the second equation and subtract it from the first, yields $$(2A_2-2A_1)x+(2B_2-2B_1)y+(2C_2-2C_1)z = c^2 (t_1-t_2) + (2 c^2 t_2 - 2 c^2 t_1) d$$

Now, we rearrange this equationsi $$0 = (2A_2-2A_1)x+(2B_2-2B_1)y+(2C_2-2C_1)z + (2 c^2 t_1 - 2 c^2 t_2) + c^2(t_2 - t_1) d $$

Subtracting equations 3 and 4 will result in equations in the same form as the above equation (but the subscripts will change):

\begin{align} 0 &= (2A_3-2A_1)x+(2B_3-2B_1)y+(2C_3-2C_1)z + (2 c^2 t_1 - 2 c^2 t_3) + c^2(t_3 - t_1) d\\ 0 &= (2A_4-2A_1)x+(2B_4-2B_1)y+(2C_4-2C_1)z + (2 c^2 t_1 - 2 c^2 t_4) + c^2(t_4 - t_1) d \end{align}

We can then establish a formula for \(x\) in terms of \(d\) from $$0=\det{\left[\vec{u}_y | \vec{u}_z | x \vec{u}_x + y \vec{u}_y + z \vec{u}_z + d \vec{u}_d + \vec{w}\right]}$$

Our earlier computations gave us the following vector components

\begin{align} \vec{u}_x &= 2 \begin{pmatrix}A_2-A_1\\A_3-A_1\\A_4-A_1\end{pmatrix}\\ \vec{u}_y &= 2 \begin{pmatrix}B_2-B_1\\B_3-B_1\\B_4-B_1\end{pmatrix}\\ \vec{u}_z &= 2 \begin{pmatrix}C_2-C_1\\C_3-C_1\\C_4-C_1\end{pmatrix}\\ \vec{u}_d &= 2 c^2 \begin{pmatrix}t_1-t_2\\t_1-t_3\\t_1-t_4\end{pmatrix}\\ \vec{w} &= c^2 \begin{pmatrix}t_2-t_1\\t_3-t_1\\t_4-t_1\end{pmatrix} \end{align}
